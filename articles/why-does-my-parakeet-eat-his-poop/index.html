Birds

Parakeets are intelligent birds that need to be stimulated in order to be happy. If owners don’t provide the right environment for their pet, they may experience behavioral abnormalities or depression.

Parakeets need a lot of stimulation to stay happy and well-adjusted. They need plenty of toys, such as chewable wood blocks, swings, bells, and ladders. They also need safe objects to chew on, such as paper or cardboard that is not toxic.

Although it looks pretty disgusting, eating its own poop can be considered a common behavior among parakeets. Despite being common, you should not allow your bird to do this.

This behavior can be caused by several reasons. In this article we will explore some of them and how you can solve them to prevent your parakeet from eating poop.

Boredom

Parakeets like to keep themselves busy when they’re bored. One way they may do this is by eating their own poop. Parakeets that don’t have enough stimulating toys may resort to this behavior.

Many parakeets in captivity are deprived of mental stimulation. This can lead to boredom and depression.

Stress

Birds are affected by stress in different ways. Some can seem unaffected while others are more vulnerable. Birds who are stressed out will need to consume more food, drink more water, and get enough sleep.

If you notice that your parakeet is stressed, they’re going to eat more than a calm, cool bird who doesn’t need extra resources.

Some of the reasons for them to get stressed out are:

  • Lack of interaction with other parakeets and people.
  • Not being fed enough.

  • Not having enough perches or toys in their cage.
