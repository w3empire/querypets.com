Why Does My Parakeet Have Diarrhea? (Vet Answers)

Birds

Parakeets are small, colorful birds that can be wonderful pets. They can be a little more high-maintenance than most other pets but the rewards they provide make it worth it.

They are also susceptible to a variety of diseases. If your parakeet starts to have diarrhea, it can be an indicator that they have a disease. You want to make sure that the bird is doing well and healthy all over.

Diarrhea is one of the most common symptoms that can be found in parakeets. This is mainly due to the fact that their digestive tract is much shorter than other animals. As a result, they are more susceptible to bacteria and parasites.

Diarrhea can come from a bacterial infection, which can be treated with antibiotics, or from an intestinal parasite, which needs to be treated with antiparasitic medication. In addition, there are many other reasons for parakeets to have diarrhea, such as: poor diet, stress and excessive water consumption, among others.

It’s important that you know what kind of diarrhea your bird has so you can treat it properly.

Diarrhea in parakeets can be caused by many things, but most of them are not life-threatening or dangerous to your pet.

Causes of Diarrhea In Parakeets

You should be aware of diarrhea symptoms in pet birds, especially when they have severe symptoms. The first thing you should do is identify what the root cause is before taking steps to reverse it.

Some of you can take care of this issue by yourself, but others require the help of a vet.

Some things that cause diarrhea in your bird are:

Fear

Parakeets often become stressed and anxious when they are in a new environment such as a new home. This may lead them to experience diarrhea.

Parakeets are usually very friendly and calm, but for those with a nervous temperament and are easily startled, the change of environment can make the bird apprehensive. However, this issue is usually resolved quickly.

This is a very common problem for new parakeets. They are not used to the new environment and they will need time to adjust.

As soon as the bird feels more comfortable with the new environment, things will fall in place.

Although these symptoms will go away in time, you’ll need to give your bird a bit of space and a chance to adjust. This may take a few days or weeks.

Parakeets are very social birds, so it is natural that they will be affected by being in an unfamiliar place.

After a Bath

This is a common issue too. You can give your parakeet a bath and its poop will be watery after. Your bird may ingest a lot of water while you are giving them a bath. It may have ingested more water than what they usually consume and this can lead to diarrhea.

In this case, the loose stools are just a way for the parakeet to get rid of all that water intake.

Your bird will soon be cured. He just needs to digest all the water that is in his body. Very soon he will be fine and healthy again.

This is usually not a serious issue. Just make sure you’re being careful when you’re giving them a bath and make sure it doesn’t drink too much water.

Fruits and Vegetables

Fruits and vegetables are considered good foods for your parakeet. But it is important to be careful with the amount he consumes.

Fruits and vegetables have a lot of water. Consumption of these foods in large quantities by your parakeet can lead to diarrhea. This happens precisely because of the large amount of water that the bird will ingest. The time he will have diarrhea is proportional to the amount of fruits and vegetables he has consumed.

Remember that the parakeet’s intestines are small and will have to process a lot of water-rich foods.

Intake of fruits and vegetables is important for your little winged friend’s health, but give him these foods in small amounts. That way you prevent him from having intestinal problems.

Molting (Changing feathers)

Molting is a natural process that many birds go through to renew their feathers and keep their plumage healthy. Sometimes, they may molt all of their feathers in one go, and it can be a very stressful process for the animal and have side effects. You can look for signs like weight loss, decreased appetite or even diarrhea.

But if your bird is eating and drinking water normally, this should not be a cause for concern.

Drink a lot of water

Parakeets are small birds that are usually seen in flocks. They have a strong social component to their lives. When they are housed together in the same place, they may start drinking competitively, which can lead to diarrhea.

Birds have a significant connection to one another. They do things together. If one of them feels like eating, the other will do the same. Even if the birds are not in the same cage, they can still exhibit this imitation behavior.

With water, is the same thing, if one drinks it, the other will drink it too, and again, and again.

This behavior can lead to a cycle where the birds continues to consume water in an exaggerated way. Even if the parakeet is no longer thirsty, it competes with other birds to see who drinks the most.

The parakeet’s organism will eventually have to eliminate all the liquid that the bird consumes and this can cause your pet to have diarrhea.

If this is the case, remove the water container from the bird’s cage for a short time. By doing this, they will have time to break out of the water drinking cycle. Thus preventing your bird from having intestinal problems.

Is Diarrhea A Sign of Illness in Parakeets?

Yes, diarrhea can be a symptom of illness in parakeets. Diarrhea is most often caused by stress, but can also be caused by diet change, parasites, or infection. The first thing you should do is take it to the vet and let them do an examination and determine the cause of the illness. They will also be able to prescribe medications and recommend a treatment plan.

Birds are susceptible to all kinds of illnesses, some of which are difficult to cure. A veterinarian is the best person to consult when your bird is ill. They will examine your bird and determine what is causing the illness.

If it is infected with some kind of virus or parasite, the bird’s body may be trying to eliminate the disease through its feces.

Although the reasons given in this short article are most likely the reasons why your parakeet has diarrhea, it is still possible that he has intestinal problems due to some illness. That’s why it’s important to consult a professional and let him evaluate your bird.

If you have already tried to solve your bird’s problem through the reasons I presented in this article and the diarrhea does not seem to improve, take your pet immediately to be consulted by a professional.

Only he will be able to determine what is causing the diarrhea and what is the best treatment for your parakeet.

How Can I Stop Parakeet Diarrhea?

We love our pets so much and as soon as they get sick we try to help them as best we can. If you notice that your parakeet is suffering from diarrhea but you are not sure what is causing the illness, a holistic approach can be a good option to solve the problem .

For starters, try to reinforce the bird’s diet. Add some components that can help in the parakeet’s balanced diet such as:

  • Hulled oatmeal and bran: It helps to absorb water from the parakeet’s intestines.
  • Kefir: A type of yogurt that can be useful in eliminating bacteria from the bird’s intestines.

These kinds of foods will be of great help for your parakeet to have more solid stools. Make sure that you are feeding your pet with the right food at this time. You can include some vitamins and electrolytes in its diet as well.

You can also try some over-the-counter medication to help your bird get better. There are tons of products to help your parakeet fight off diarrhea and restore their digestive health.

But remember, it is always important to consult a vet before offering anything to your parakeet.

Is there anything else I can do to stop parakeet diarrhea?

You can remove whatever is causing the diarrhea from the parakeet’s environment. If after observing your pet’s behavior you are able to identify what is making him sick, just remove this element from the environment where he is.

If he is anxious or stressed, try to provide solutions to calm him down or make him feel more comfortable. If he is drinking a lot of water, temporarily remove the bowl of water from his cage.

Food can be one of the causes of diarrhea as well. See if the bird is eating something different, something that could cause loose stools, and remove that from its diet.

When is Parakeet Diarrhea a Problem?

Parakeet diarrhea can become a serious problem. The thing is, when we think about things like diarrhea in humans, we usually think of the worst case scenario. But when it comes to parakeets, it’s not always so bad.

Usually in two days your parakeet’s diarrhea will have improved. If it doesn’t, take him to a vet. He could be suffering from an infection.

Most of the time, when a parakeet has diarrhea, it’s just because they ate something that didn’t agree with them or they had too much water too quickly. That said, if you’re noticing an increased amount of droppings in the cage and you’re seeing blood in them (or any other unusual color), then you should take the bird to a veterinarian as soon as possible.

It is important to take care of your bird. If your parakeet has had diarrhea for more than two days, he may need some kind of medication or antibiotic to help him.

Most people don’t know that parakeets can get dehydrated. Leaving the diarrhea untreated can lead to dehydration which can result in a number of serious health problems.

Some symptoms that may indicate that your parakeet is suffering from an infection:

  • Weight loss,
  • Sunken eyes,
  • Tremors.

If you notice these symptoms in the bird, it’s time to pay a visit to the vet to get it checked out.

Understanding Parakeet Diarrhea

Diarrhea is a common ailment in parakeets, and it should not be cause for concern. Most parakeets will have one or two loose stools in a day, but this is normal. If the condition persists, or if there is blood in the stool or fecal matter stuck to the feathers around the bird’s vent, consult a veterinarian.

If the bird is exhibiting behaviors like those listed in this article, it is normal for the bird to have diarrhea. Eating foods rich in water like fruits and vegetables in excess or drinking too much water will cause your parakeet to have intestinal problems.

In cases like these, as soon as your pet’s body removes the excess water, the bird will feel better.

Conclusion and summary

In this short article you learned why your parakeet has diarrhea.

One of the most common reasons a parakeet has diarrhea is stress. Stress in the bird is usually caused by changes in environment, very loud noises, the presence of other animals in the same environment as the parakeet or when it is being handled all the time by humans.

The parakeet can also have diarrhea from consuming too much water, either from consuming foods such as fruits and vegetables or from the influence of other birds drinking water, if one bird drinks water another bird will mimic this behavior which can lead to an exaggerated water consumption cycle.

In addition, if he has diarrhea for more than two days, it may be an indication of an infection, in which case it is important to take him to the vet immediately.
