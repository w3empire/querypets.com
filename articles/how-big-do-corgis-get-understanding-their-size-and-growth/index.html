How Big Do Corgis Get? Understanding Their Size and Growth

author Annika Sjögren

Corgis, with their adorable looks and charismatic personalities, have captured the hearts of dog lovers around the world. These pint-sized pups are known for their distinct appearance, short legs, and long bodies.

However, one question that often arises in the minds of potential Corgi owners is: “How big do Corgis actually get?” In this article, we will delve into the world of Corgi sizes, exploring their growth potential and shedding light on the different stages of their development.

How Big Do Corgis Get?

Corgis, both Pembroke and Cardigan breeds, typically reach a height of 10 to 12 inches (25 to 30 centimeters) at the shoulder and weigh between 25 and 38 pounds (11 to 17 kilograms) as adults. This is the general size range you can expect for fully grown Corgis.

It’s important to note that individual Corgis may fall slightly outside this average range due to factors such as genetics, nutrition, exercise, and gender. Male Corgis tend to be slightly larger than females, with males weighing between 27 and 38 pounds (12 to 17 kilograms) and females weighing between 25 and 34 pounds (11 to 15 kilograms).

Remember, the size range mentioned applies to both the Pembroke and Cardigan breeds, which are the two main breeds of Corgis.

Now that we’ve directly answered the question about the size of Corgis, let’s explore the growth stages, factors influencing size, and other relevant information to provide a comprehensive understanding of Corgi sizes.

Corgi Breeds

Before we dive into more details about the specifics of Corgi sizes, it’s important to understand that there are two main breeds of Corgis: the Pembroke Welsh Corgi and the Cardigan Welsh Corgi. While both breeds share similarities in terms of appearance and temperament, there are notable differences between them.

Pembroke Welsh Corgis are more popular and slightly smaller than their Cardigan counterparts. They have a distinct foxy face, erect ears, and come in various coat colors, including red, sable, and tri-color.

On the other hand, Cardigan Welsh Corgis have a more robust build, larger ears, and a long tail. They also come in a wider range of coat colors, including brindle, blue merle, and red merle.

When it comes to size, it’s important to note that the size range discussed in this article applies to both the Pembroke and Cardigan breeds.

Growth Stages

To understand Corgi sizes comprehensively, we need to explore the different stages of their growth. Just like humans, Corgis go through three main stages: puppyhood, adolescence, and adulthood. Each stage comes with its own set of changes and considerations.

During all these stages, proper nutrition, exercise, and regular veterinary care play a crucial role in ensuring healthy growth and development.

Puppy Size

Corgi puppies are undeniably one of the cutest sights in the world. At birth, they are tiny bundles of joy, weighing an average of 10 to 12 ounces (280 to 340 grams). Over the first few weeks of their lives, they grow rapidly, both in size and in their ability to explore the world around them.

By the time Corgi puppies are ready to leave their mother and join their forever homes, they typically weigh between 8 and 12 pounds (3.6 and 5.4 kilograms) and stand around 10 to 12 inches (25 to 30 centimeters) tall at the shoulder.

It’s important to remember that these numbers can vary, and individual puppies may fall slightly outside this range.

Adolescent Size

Once Corgi puppies enter adolescence, they experience a growth spurt that takes them closer to their adult size. This period usually occurs between the ages of 4 months and 12 months. During this time, Corgis can grow rapidly in both height and weight.

On average, adolescent Corgis reach their maximum height and weight by the time they are 9 to 12 months old.

At this stage, they typically weigh between 20 and 30 pounds (9 and 13.6 kilograms) and stand around 10 to 12 inches (25 to 30 centimeters) tall at the shoulder. Again, it’s important to note that individual Corgis may fall slightly outside these averages.

Adult Size

As Corgis transition into adulthood, their growth rate slows down, and they reach their full size. On average, fully grown Corgis stand around 10 to 12 inches (25 to 30 centimeters) tall at the shoulder and weigh between 25 and 38 pounds (11 to 17 kilograms).

The size range mentioned applies to both the Pembroke and Cardigan breeds. However, it’s worth mentioning that there can be slight differences in size between individual Corgis, depending on factors such as genetics and gender.

In terms of gender differences, male Corgis tend to be slightly larger than females, both in height and weight. Males may weigh between 27 and 38 pounds (12 and 17 kilograms), while females may weigh between 25 and 34 pounds (11 and 15 kilograms).

Factors Affecting Size

Several factors can influence the size of a Corgi. Genetics play a significant role, as the size of the parents can give a good indication of the potential size of their offspring. Responsible breeders strive to maintain breed standards and produce Corgis within a certain size range.

Proper nutrition is also crucial to ensuring healthy growth. Providing a balanced diet that meets the nutritional needs of a growing Corgi is essential. Overfeeding or underfeeding can lead to weight issues and potentially impact their overall size.

Regular exercise is equally important. Corgis have a propensity to gain weight, and engaging them in physical activities helps maintain a healthy size and prevents obesity-related health issues.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Corgis are captivating dogs that bring immense joy to their owners. When it comes to their size, Corgis fall within a specific range at each stage of their lives. From the adorable size of a newborn puppy to the fully grown stature of an adult Corgi, their growth potential is fascinating to witness.

Aspiring Corgi owners should consider the breed’s size and ensure they can provide appropriate care and space for these wonderful dogs. Understanding the different growth stages and the factors that influence size is crucial to raising a healthy and happy Corgi companion.

So, if you’re captivated by the charm of Corgis and have fallen in love with their adorable appearance, be prepared for a dog that will grow into a small to medium-sized bundle of joy. With the right care and love, your Corgi will bring endless smiles and happiness to your life.
